Ferrari 330 P3 (1966): The Ferrari 330 P3 was introduced in 1966 as a successor to the 330 P2. It featured a 4.0-liter V12 engine and was designed for endurance racing. The P3 competed in events like the 24 Hours of Le Mans, where it had notable success.

    Evolution of the Ferrari Race Series Cars

  • Ferrari 330 P4 (1967):

    • The 330 P4 was an evolution of the P3, featuring improvements in aerodynamics and engine performance.
    • It is one of the most iconic racing cars produced by Ferrari and is often considered one of the most beautiful racing cars of all time.
    • While it didn't secure an overall victory at Le Mans in 1967, it did finish 1-2-3 at the 1967 24 Hours of Daytona and had other successes in endurance racing.
    • The P4 continued to compete in endurance racing, and a few examples are still highly sought after by collectors.

  • Ferrari 312 P (1969-1970):

    • The Ferrari 312 P was developed as a response to the changing regulations in sports car racing.
    • It featured a 3.0-liter V12 engine and had successes in races like the 24 Hours of Daytona.
    • The 312 P marked the transition to using engines derived from Formula One cars for endurance racing.
    • The 312 PB is considered one of the most successful prototypes in Ferrari's history.

  • Ferrari 333 SP (1994-2001):

    • While not directly part of the P series, the 333 SP is worth mentioning as it marked Ferrari's return to prototype sports car racing.
    • It featured a 4.0-liter V12 engine and was particularly successful in endurance racing in the United States.
    • While not as prominent in international endurance racing, the 333 SP was highly successful in North America, winning multiple championships and the 24 Hours of Daytona.

  • Ferrari 550 Maranello (2001-2005):

    • The Ferrari 550 Maranello achieved notable success at the 24 Hours of Le Mans in 2003, securing a 1-2 victory in the GTS class.
    • The winning car, fielded by Prodrive, was driven by Darren Turner, Rickard Rydell, and Colin McRae.
    • Engine: 5.5-liter V12. Class: Competed in the GTS (GT2) class.

  • Ferrari F430 GT2 (2006-2010):

    • The F430 GT2, campaigned by Risi Competizione, claimed victory in the GT2 class at the 2008 24 Hours of Le Mans.
    • The winning driver lineup included Jamie Melo, Mika Salo, and Gianmaria Bruni.
    • Engine: 4.0-liter V8. Class: Competed in the GT2 class.

  • Ferrari 458 Italia GTE (2011-2015):

    • The 458 Italia GTE achieved success in the GTE Pro class at the 2012 24 Hours of Le Mans, marking Ferrari's return to the top step of the podium at Le Mans after a 14-year hiatus.
    • AF Corse fielded the winning car with drivers Giancarlo Fisichella, Gianmaria Bruni, and Toni Vilander.
    • Engine: Twin-turbocharged 3.9-liter V8. Class: Continued in the GTE Pro and GTE Am classes.
